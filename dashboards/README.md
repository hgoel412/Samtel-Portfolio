# Dashboard Gallery

Each chart covers one layer of the study. All are generated by the scripts in `analysis/` from the STK exports in `data/raw/`.

## 1. Revisit per Asset
**File:** `01_revisit.png`

Worst-case and typical revisit at each asset, all five constellations:
- P50 bar (typical) with P95 whisker (realistic worst case)
- The five assets: two EEZ points, Ladakh, Siachen, Tawang
- Shows SSO failing at ~2.5 hours, C1 fixing the border but not the sea, C3 and C4 under ~14 minutes everywhere

**Audience:** Technical reviewers, mission planners

---

## 2. End-to-End Latency
**Files:** `02_latency_sovereign.png`, `03_latency_global.png`, `04_max_latency_sovereign.png`

Time from collection to ground-station downlink, per asset and constellation:
- 02: India-only (sovereign) ground segment
- 03: global integrated network
- 04: absolute worst-case sovereign latency
- The gap between sovereign and global is the cost of keeping data on Indian soil; the western maritime tail is visible here

**Audience:** Operations, ground-segment planning

---

## 3. Orbital Lifetime and Decay
**Files:** `05_lifetime_vs_altitude.png`, `06_decay_profile_design_window.png`

Service life and disposal compliance versus altitude:
- 05: nominal lifetime with the ±2σ solar-flux band, drag-area brackets, the 5-year disposal rule, and the design window
- 06: unattended decay profiles at 525 and 550 km — apogee and perigee from launch to re-entry
- The orbit decays circular; disposal is an altitude-and-drag decision

**Audience:** Mission assurance, disposal compliance

---

## 4. Δv Budget
**File:** `07_dv_budget.png`

Per-satellite propulsion sizing:
- Δv stacked by component (deorbit, station-keeping, phasing, injection, collision avoidance, 25% margin) at 525 and 550 km for 5- and 7-year lives
- Propellant mass on the 4 kg bus for cold-gas, green-monopropellant, and electric options

**Audience:** Propulsion, systems

---

## 5. Resilience
**File:** `08_degradation_resilience.png`

Graceful degradation under satellite failures:
- Worst-asset revisit and sovereign latency (P95) as 0 to 8 satellites are removed, for C3 and C4
- Median line with the 10th-to-90th-percentile band
- The first loss is invisible; the jump is at the second loss

**Audience:** Reliability, replenishment planning

---

## 6. Spatial Revisit Fields
**Files:** `09_fom_heatmaps.png`, `10_fom_latitude_profile.png`

Coverage structure across the theatre:
- 09: five-panel worst-case revisit heatmaps, with assets and Indian ground stations marked
- 10: zonal (per-latitude) worst-case revisit, showing each constellation's latitude structure and the equatorial revisit hole in C3 and C4

**Audience:** Coverage analysts, mission design
